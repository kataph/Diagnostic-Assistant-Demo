We define a diagnostic action as any action that service personnel may perform on a component while addressing an equipment failure.

To simplify maintenance knowledge management, diagnostic actions are grouped into a reduced set of categories. Each category is associated with a relative cost that reflects the expected effort, time, and resource usage. A diagnostic action belongs to one of the following four types:

 - Replace: swapping a component with another component.
(cost = 10)

 - Adjust: adjusting, refitting, tuning, reconfiguring, or repairing a component without replacing it.
(cost = 5)

 - Test: testing or measuring a component using tools and/or by actively manipulating it.
(cost = 3)

 - Observe: visually inspecting or analyzing a component without tools and without physically manipulating it.
(cost = 1)
